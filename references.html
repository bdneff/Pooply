Medical Sources & References

Last updated: June 2026

Pooply is for general wellness and educational purposes only. It is not a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, or prevent any condition. Its analysis is an estimate based on a photo and the published references below — it is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider about your health, and seek care promptly for symptoms such as blood in your stool, black/tarry stools, severe pain, or diarrhea lasting more than 48 hours.

The stool classifications, color interpretations, and diet/hydration guidance shown in Pooply's analysis are based on the following published clinical and public-health sources.

1. Stool form classification (Bristol Stool Scale)

Pooply classifies each log into one of seven Bristol Stool Scale types and maps those to general transit-time interpretations (constipation, healthy, loose/diarrhea).

Primary source — the original validated scale

3. Blood in stool

When Pooply flags possible blood (overt red streaking or black/tarry appearance), it advises seeking medical attention. This is informational and not a diagnosis.
